FurryTails | What Foods Are Unsafe for Cats? A Must-Read for New Cat Owners

As cat owners, we all want to provide the best care for our furry friends. However, a cat’s dietary needs are quite different from ours. Some foods that are delicious for us can be dangerous, even deadly, for cats. Here are some foods that cats should avoid and the reasons why. This is essential information for new cat owners!

Q1. Can Cats Drink Milk?

No, cats lack the enzyme lactase needed to break down lactose, which can lead to digestive issues and diarrhea. Instead, offer cat-specific milk, unsweetened yogurt, unsweetened and unflavored kefir, or natural cheese balls to supplement calcium.

Q2. Can Cats Eat Vegetables?

Certain vegetables are harmful to cats:

  • Onions, Scallions, Chives, Shallots, and Garlic: These can destroy a cat’s red blood cells, leading to anemia. Ingesting 600–800 grams can cause acute poisoning.
  • Eggplant and Spinach: Contain oxalic acid, which can cause urinary tract stones in cats.
  • Potatoes and Carrots: High in carbohydrates, excessive consumption can lead to obesity and metabolic diseases. These can be given occasionally as side dishes but not in large quantities.

Q3. Can Cats Eat Fruits?

Avoid giving cats the following fruits:

  • Grapes: Can cause kidney failure.
  • Avocados: Can be toxic if consumed in large amounts.
  • Cherries: Contain alkaloids, which are toxic.
  • Fruit Seeds: Contain cyanide, which can cause severe poisoning and even shock (e.g., apples, apricots, peaches).
  • Citrus Fruits: Highly acidic, can cause vomiting and allergic reactions.

Always remove seeds and pits from fruits to prevent choking and cyanide poisoning.

Q4. Can Cats Chew on Bones?

Cooked bones should be avoided as they can splinter and puncture a cat’s esophagus or gastrointestinal tract. You can give frozen poultry necks or wings, which are relatively safe. The high acidity in a cat’s stomach can digest these small bones, but always supervise to ensure they don’t swallow large pieces whole.

Q5. Can Cats Eat Chocolate?

Chocolate is a definite no-go for cats! Theobromine and caffeine in chocolate can cause severe toxicity in cats.

By understanding and avoiding these harmful foods, we can help our cats lead healthier, longer lives. This guide is crucial for new cat owners to ensure the well-being of their beloved pets.
