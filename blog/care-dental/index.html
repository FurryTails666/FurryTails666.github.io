Dog Dental Chews: Starting and Choosing Guide

As dog owners become more aware of the importance of oral health for their pets, dental chews have emerged as a popular tool for maintaining a dog’s teeth and gums. But how do you use dental chews correctly? What should you look for when choosing the right product? This guide answers common questions and offers practical advice to ensure your dog’s dental care is on the right track.

Q1. When Should You Start Using Dental Chews for Your Dog?

Introducing dental chews early in your dog’s life can help establish good dental habits. It’s recommended to start brushing your dog’s teeth from a young age to get them used to the sensation. Begin feeding dental chews once their permanent teeth have emerged, usually around six months old. At this stage, dental chews can support brushing by cleaning hard-to-reach areas, particularly the molars.

  • Tip: Start with soft, puppy-friendly chews and gradually transition to harder chews as your dog matures.

Q2. Are Dental Chews Necessary for Dog Dental Health?

Yes, dental chews can significantly enhance your dog’s oral health by reducing plaque and tartar buildup. While brushing remains the gold standard for dog dental care, dental chews provide additional cleaning and freshening benefits. For best results, choose products certified by the Veterinary Oral Health Council (VOHC), ensuring they meet rigorous dental health standards.

  • Important: Chews without VOHC certification may not provide the same benefits, so it’s essential to read the label carefully before purchasing.

Q3. How Often Should You Give Your Dog Dental Chews?

Dental chews can be given once a day, but if you offer more than one, be sure to reduce your dog’s regular food intake to avoid overfeeding. As treats, dental chews should not make up more than 10% of your dog’s daily caloric intake. Adjusting your dog’s diet to account for these calories is crucial to prevent weight gain.

  • Tip: Balance dental chews with your dog’s daily meals to maintain a healthy diet.

Q4. Should Dental Chews Be Given Before or After Brushing?

Dental chews can be given before or after brushing, but many owners prefer offering them after brushing as a reward. This timing helps reinforce positive dental habits and provides a final clean of your dog’s mouth.

  • Tip: Make dental chews part of your dog’s routine to encourage compliance with brushing.

Q5. How to Choose the Right Dental Chews for Your Dog

When selecting a dental chew, there are a few factors to consider:

  • Size: Always choose a chew appropriate for your dog’s size. Larger dogs require larger chews to avoid choking hazards, while smaller dogs need smaller chews that are easy to handle.
  • Hardness: The chew should not be too hard. A good test is to press your thumbnail into the chew—if it leaves an impression, it’s the right hardness. Chews that are too hard can cause dental fractures.
  • VOHC Certification: Opt for products with VOHC certification to ensure they are effective in maintaining oral health.

Q6. How to Feed Dental Chews Properly

Dental chews are most effective when chewed using the back teeth. This mechanical action helps clean the molars and reduce tartar. If your dog tends to swallow chews whole, you can hold one end while they gnaw on the other to ensure they chew thoroughly before swallowing.

  • Tip: Supervise your dog when feeding dental chews to ensure safe consumption.

Q7. Are Dental Chews Effective If They Break Easily?

Yes, dental chews are effective even if they break in half quickly. The mechanical action of chewing, whether on a whole or broken piece, helps clean teeth by scraping off plaque. As long as your dog chews, the product is fulfilling its purpose.

Q8. What to Do If Bad Breath Persists Despite Brushing and Dental Chews?

Persistent bad breath could indicate underlying dental issues such as infections or abscesses. If your dog’s breath remains bad despite regular brushing and dental chews, it’s essential to consult your vet for a thorough checkup. They can identify any oral health issues and recommend further treatment.

Comprehensive Tips for Dog Dental Health

Maintaining your dog’s dental health is about consistency. Brushing daily, using dental chews, and scheduling regular veterinary dental checkups all work together to prevent issues like periodontal disease. Dental chews are a great complement to brushing, helping to remove plaque and freshen breath between brushings.
