FurryTails | Essential Guide for Taking Care of Puppies: Tips and Product Recommendations for New Dog Owners

Introduction

Puppies are irresistibly cute and endearing, making many people eager to raise them from a young age. However, just like newborn babies, puppies are delicate and require careful attention. Without proper care, they can easily fall ill, which can impact their future health. This guide provides essential tips and product recommendations for first-time dog owners raising very young puppies.

Nursing Period (Birth to 1 Month)

Newborn puppies need significant care, including feeding, assisting with excretion, and monitoring their health. During this period, they are extremely fragile, especially if they do not have access to their mother’s milk. Here’s how to ensure they get the proper nutrition and care:

1. What Should Nursing Puppies Eat? Can They Drink Cow’s Milk?

  • Mother’s Milk: The ideal food for puppies is their mother’s milk, which offers essential proteins, fats, and antibodies to strengthen their immune system. If the mother’s milk is unavailable, a special puppy formula is a suitable alternative.
  • Special Formula Milk: Designed specifically for puppies, this formula is easy to digest, preventing diarrhea and ensuring proper nutrition.

2. Feeding Techniques for Puppies Under One Month

  • Daily Intake: Puppies require approximately 180 ml/kg of milk per day, divided equally into meals.
  • Feeding Method: Hold the puppy face down with its head slightly raised. Use a bottle with a small hole in the nipple to dispense milk drop by drop to avoid choking.

3. Assisting Puppies with Urination and Defecation

  • Stimulating Excretion: Puppies up to three weeks old need stimulation to urinate and defecate. If the mother is not present, use a clean tissue dipped in warm water to gently wipe their anus and urethra.

Weaning Period (1 to 2 Months)

Around one month old, puppies begin teething and can start transitioning to solid food.

1. Recommended Weaning Foods

  • Meat Paste: A soft texture makes it easy for puppies to lick and digest. Mix with formula milk to ease the transition.
  • Soaked Dry Food: Choose high-protein, low-carb dry food that is easy to digest.
  • Puppy Food Cans: Nutritious and easy to mix with milk for a smooth weaning process.

2. Weaning Transition Guide

  • Gradual Introduction: Start by adding a small amount of weaning food to the formula milk and gradually increase the amount until it completely replaces the formula. If digestive issues arise, reduce the weaning food temporarily.

3. Deworming and Vaccination

  • Deworming: Consult a veterinarian to start deworming around 2-3 weeks old. Some deworming products are suitable from two months old.
  • Vaccination: Begin the first set of vaccinations around 6-9 weeks old, followed by booster shots every 2-4 weeks, with the final dose after 16 weeks.

Growth Period (3 to 9 Months)

During this period, puppies experience rapid growth and teething.

1. Puppy Teething Period

  • Teething Challenges: Around three months, puppies’ baby teeth fall out as permanent teeth grow in. Provide durable chew toys to help alleviate discomfort.
  • Recommended Chew Toys: Select toys that are appropriately sized, made from safe, durable materials like undecorated plush toys and dedicated chew sticks.

2. Puppy Diet Guide

  • Nutritional Needs: This stage requires ample nutrition to support growth and development. Opt for specially formulated puppy food, such as puppy kibble and main food cans.

3. Common Diet Misconceptions

  • Homemade Fresh Food: May lack proper nutritional balance and lead to deficiencies. Commercially prepared foods are formulated to meet balanced nutritional needs.
  • Calcium Supplementation: Excessive calcium can cause joint issues. Ensure that the calcium ratio in the food meets the puppy’s needs.

Conclusion

By understanding the care requirements at different growth stages and choosing the right products, new dog owners can ensure their puppies grow healthy and happy. Proper care during these formative months sets the foundation for a well-adjusted, thriving adult dog.
